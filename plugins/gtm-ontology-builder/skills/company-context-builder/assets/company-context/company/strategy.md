# Company strategy

## Mission

State the durable progress the company exists to enable. Keep this distinct from a
tagline, product feature list, or unverified outcome claim.

## Target-market choices

Describe the markets the company deliberately serves and excludes. Keep narrower
customer situations, champions, and qualification criteria in product-group files.

## Portfolio posture

Explain the role of each product group, the current phase of the portfolio, and the
conditions under which the company would add, expand, contract, or stop a group.

## Shared narrative

Define the company-level comparison frame and narrative boundaries that every
product group inherits without forcing distinct groups into one positioning.

## Strategic choices

List the few explicit choices that govern product, market, and route-to-market
decisions. Include what the company is choosing not to do.

## GTM constraints

Record material capacity, delivery, channel, evidence, or claim constraints shared
across the portfolio.

## Decision horizon

State the planning horizon and triggers for earlier review.

## Shared differentiation

Record only company-wide, evidenced differentiators. Keep group-specific comparison
chains in each group's positioning artifact.
