# Commerce Analytics strategy

## Strategic role

Commerce Analytics is Acme Analytics' established entry product group. It turns
first-party commerce data into governed recurring analysis for commercial teams
that have outgrown storefront reports and spreadsheet reconciliation but do not
want to build a fully custom analytics stack.

The group specializes the company strategy around one customer situation. It does
not target every commerce company, analytics buyer, or organization with customer
data.

## GTM phase

**Current phase:** expansion.

The group has a defined segment, bounded offers, and an established sales-led
motion. Expansion means increasing adoption from one commerce team to broader
multi-team governance inside the same core market. It does not authorize expansion
into unrelated industries, generic BI use cases, or new channels without a separate
strategy review.

## Primary market

- **Segment:** `segment:commerce-analytics-core-segment`.
- **Primary use case:** `use-case:commerce-analytics-core-use-case`.
- **Typical champion:** Head of E-commerce or equivalent commercial owner.
- **Economic buyer:** the champion for a bounded one-team purchase; commonly a CMO
  or executive sponsor when scope crosses teams, sources, or markets.
- **Primary current ways:** storefront reports, recurring spreadsheets, and custom
  warehouse-and-BI analysis.

The segment qualifies through workflow need, ownership, and operational readiness.
Revenue, headcount, region, industry, or job title may prioritize a qualified
organization but never establish market participation by themselves.

## Offer architecture

- **Growth** is the entry offer for one primary commerce team, standard sources,
  and a bounded recurring analysis workflow.
- **Scale** expands the same core use case across multiple teams, sources, markets,
  or governance requirements.

Growth and Scale are packages within one market. Route by evidenced complexity and
adoption scope, not budget alone. A need for governed downstream activation belongs
to the Data Activation group rather than a hidden Commerce Analytics customization.

## Primary motion

The primary motion is sales-led inbound education followed by discovery and a data-fit
assessment. Targeted outbound is a secondary route for visible high-intensity
signals, especially multi-team or multi-market situations. Both routes use the same
qualification order and require an accountable workflow owner.

## Constraints

- No unguided self-service purchase while metric ownership and data readiness need
  human validation.
- No claim of guaranteed revenue growth, attribution accuracy, or automated
  decision-making.
- No offer may imply that Acme operates the customer's warehouse, storefront, or
  financial reporting system.
- Onboarding remains bounded by supported sources, explicit metric validation, and
  named customer owners.
- Adjacent use cases do not enter the roadmap or messaging until the primary use
  case remains repeatable without unpriced custom work.

## Measures and gates

Review qualified discovery rate, data-fit completion, proposal conversion, days to
decision, onboarding plan changes, and adoption of the agreed recurring workflow.

Continue expansion when the same segment, use case, qualification logic, and offer
boundaries remain valid across wins. Revisit the strategy when product recommendations
frequently change during onboarding, custom work becomes necessary to close deals,
the champion cannot own adoption, or buyers consistently evaluate a different use
case or current alternative.
