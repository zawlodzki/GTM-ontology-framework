# Commerce Analytics positioning

## Category

Governed commerce analytics for mid-market brands.

## Positioning statement

For commerce teams that have outgrown storefront reports and spreadsheet
reconciliation, Commerce Analytics provides trusted customer, product, and revenue
insight without requiring a custom analytics stack. Unlike basic reporting tools,
it makes metric definitions and drill-down explicit; unlike a bespoke warehouse
project, it starts with opinionated commerce workflows and a bounded onboarding path.

## Value pillars

1. **Trusted numbers:** documented definitions, source traceability, and ownership.
2. **Faster decisions:** recurring workflows for trading, retention, and planning.
3. **Progressive depth:** start with standard commerce analysis and expand governance as needed.
4. **Shared context:** operators and executives work from the same underlying model.

## Relevant alternatives

| Alternative | Why buyers consider it | Positioning response |
|---|---|---|
| Storefront reports | Already available and easy to use | Useful for basic monitoring, limited for cross-source definitions and deeper cohorts |
| Spreadsheets | Flexible and familiar | Flexible, but costly to reconcile, audit, and repeat across teams |
| Custom warehouse and BI | Maximum control and extensibility | Strong fit for staffed data teams; slower when the immediate need is governed commerce analysis |
| Broad enterprise suite | One vendor for many capabilities | Broad coverage may add implementation and administration beyond the defined use case |

## Product distinction

- **Growth** is positioned around replacing recurring manual analysis for one core commerce team.
- **Scale** is positioned around shared governance and broader adoption across teams, sources, or markets.

## Message guardrails

- Do not promise revenue growth, perfect attribution, or automated decision-making.
- Do not describe the product as a data warehouse, financial system, or marketing platform.
- Do not claim setup is effortless; state customer data and validation responsibilities.
- Use fictional competitor profiles only as structural examples, not as market evidence.
