# Data Activation positioning

## Category

Governed warehouse data activation for commerce organizations.

## Positioning statement

For data and lifecycle teams that already trust their warehouse models but struggle
to operationalize them safely, Data Activation delivers approved customer data to
business systems without maintaining destination-specific pipelines. Unlike manual
exports or custom scripts, it provides repeatable mappings, monitoring, recovery,
and access controls while leaving the warehouse as the source of truth.

## Value pillars

1. **Warehouse authority:** use approved models instead of creating another customer model.
2. **Controlled speed:** shorten business-team wait times without bypassing governance.
3. **Operational evidence:** make mappings, runs, failures, and recovery visible.
4. **Bounded adoption:** begin with one owned use case and expand only after acceptance.

## Relevant alternatives

| Alternative | Why buyers consider it | Positioning response |
|---|---|---|
| Custom pipelines | Maximum control using existing engineering tools | Flexible, but each destination adds maintenance, monitoring, and ownership cost |
| Manual exports | Fast for a one-time request | Difficult to repeat, govern, suppress, or audit in production |
| Broad customer data platform | Identity, collection, and activation in one suite | Useful when a new central platform is required; excessive when the warehouse model is already authoritative |
| Native destination connectors | Included with an engagement tool | Convenient for limited sources, less suitable for governed cross-destination definitions |

## Product distinction

- **Warehouse Sync** is positioned for technical teams delivering modeled attributes or records.
- **Audience Activation** is positioned for lifecycle teams publishing governed segments from approved data.

## Message guardrails

- Do not describe the products as a warehouse, consent system, identity provider, or campaign tool.
- Do not promise perfect identity resolution, instant delivery, or error-free destinations.
- Do not imply product-led access permits unsupervised production activation.
- Do not present reduced engineering work as eliminating technical ownership.
