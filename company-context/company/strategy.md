# Company strategy

## Mission

Help digital commerce teams make first-party customer data useful without forcing
every team to build and maintain its own analytics infrastructure.

## Company-wide positioning

Acme Analytics sits between basic storefront reporting and fully custom data
platforms. It provides opinionated workflows that are faster to adopt than a
bespoke stack while preserving the governance, exports, and integration paths
required by data-mature organizations.

## Strategic choices

1. Serve defined commerce use cases instead of becoming a general-purpose BI tool.
2. Package capabilities around distinct buying contexts and product groups.
3. Make trustworthy definitions and traceable data more important than dashboard volume.
4. Support progressive adoption, from a managed analytics workspace to governed activation.
5. Integrate with existing commerce, warehouse, and engagement systems rather than replace them.

## Shared differentiation

- Predefined commerce metrics and workflows shorten time to value.
- Business users can explore governed data without writing queries.
- Data teams retain control through documented definitions, exports, and access rules.
- Product groups share a common data model while addressing different buyers and motions.

Group-specific positioning and proof belong in each product group's
`go-to-market/positioning.md`, not in this company-wide artifact.
