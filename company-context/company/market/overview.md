# Market overview

## Category

Acme Analytics operates in commerce intelligence and customer-data activation
software. The category connects behavioral and transaction data with analysis,
segmentation, and downstream engagement workflows.

## Market boundaries

The company overlaps with product analytics, e-commerce reporting, customer data
platforms, warehouse-native activation, and business intelligence. It does not
compete as a general data warehouse, marketing automation suite, or consulting-led
data transformation provider.

## Demand drivers

- Privacy changes make reliable first-party data more valuable.
- Commerce teams need consistent customer and revenue definitions across tools.
- Business users want faster answers without waiting for a central data team.
- Data teams want governed activation without uncontrolled exports or shadow pipelines.
- Buyers increasingly prefer products that complement their existing warehouse and engagement stack.

## Common alternatives

Organizations may continue using storefront dashboards and spreadsheets, assemble
a custom warehouse and BI stack, buy a broad customer data platform, or outsource
analysis to an agency. The relevant alternative depends on the product group and
must be evaluated in that group's positioning artifact.

## Evaluation dimensions

Buyers typically compare time to value, metric trust, source coverage, activation
destinations, governance, implementation effort, total cost, and the amount of
specialist support required after launch.
